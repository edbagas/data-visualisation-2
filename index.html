Global Internet Users Rate

The internet has become an integral part of today's world. As of January 2021, there are 4.66 billion active internet users, 59.5 percent of the global population. With every countries, we take a deep look into how some factors (average income, population, overall happiness, GDP, energy consumption) affect the rate of internet users around the world.

The map above shows how the Internet Users Rate differ with each country, normalised per 100 people. The darker colors represent a higher Internet Users Rate, while the lighter colors represent a lower Internet Users Rate. From this map, we can see that Europe generally has the highest rate of internet users, while Africa generally has the lowest.


How does Income affect Internet Users Rate?

The plot above shows the relationship between the average Income in a country and the Internet Users Rate. We can see that it has a positive correlation, meaning that a country's Internet Users Rate generally increases if the average Income increases.

The size of the plots are dictated by the Urban Rate of the country, which is the percentage of a country's population that lives in urban areas.

We can also see that our previous statement was correct, that Europe generally has a higher Internet Users Rate while Africa generally has a lower Internet Users Rate.


How does other factors affect Internet Users Rate?

The plots above show how Population, Happiness, and Gross Domestic Product (GDP) affect the Internet Users Rate. Since the internet users rate is scaled, we do not see any relationship with Population.

With Happiness, we can see that there is a strong positive correlation. This means that generally as Happiness increases, the Internet Users Rate generally increases too.

The GDP of a country is generally used to describe the economics of a country. Comparing GDP and the Internet Users Rate, the GDP does not seem to have any strong relationship with the Internet Users Rate, meaning that GDP does not have not an effect towards the Internet Users Rate.


Top 20 Countries with the Highest Energy Usage

The map above shows the Total Energy Usage of the highest 20 countries. The darker colors represent a higher Total Energy Usage, while the lighter colors represent a lower Total Energy Usage. From this map, we can see that China generally has the highest Total Energy Usage.


More internet users mean more energy usage, right?

The chart above shows the breakdown of each country's energy usage, and is sorted by the highest to the lowest Internet Usage Rate (left to right)

From this, we can see how it turns out how the Total Energy Usage does NOT have a relationship with the Internet Usage Rate. This is quite a surprise, since we would have assumed that the more internet users there are, the more technological advanced a country is and hence would increase the Total Energy Usage.
